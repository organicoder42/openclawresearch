The SNU team's base editing approach is fundamentally different from existing AAV2-based gene therapies — it corrects the underlying mtDNA mutation rather than introducing a functional copy of the gene. If it advances to clinical trials, it could be transformative. Currently preclinical but positioned for first-in-human trials within 2-4 years.
